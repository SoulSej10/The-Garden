# Purpose

Learning defines how intelligent agents improve through experience.

Every interaction with the world has the potential to modify future behavior.

Learning allows individuals to acquire knowledge, refine skills, adapt strategies, avoid repeated mistakes, and transmit discoveries to others.

Without learning, intelligence remains static.

With learning, individuals—and eventually civilizations—continuously evolve.

---

# Scientific Basis

Learning is the process by which experience produces relatively lasting changes in knowledge or behavior.

Learning occurs through observation, experimentation, repetition, feedback, social interaction, and reflection.

The Garden models learning as an ongoing adaptive process embedded within everyday life.

# Closing Statement

A child failed to start a fire.

The next day, they tried again.

An explorer became lost.

Years later, they guided others safely along the same path.

A healer mixed the wrong herbs.

In The Garden, every mistake holds the possibility of wisdom, and every success becomes the beginning of something greater.
