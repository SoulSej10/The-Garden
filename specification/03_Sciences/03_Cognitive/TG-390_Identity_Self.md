# Purpose

Identity & Self defines how intelligent individuals develop an enduring understanding of who they are.

Identity emerges gradually through lived experience.

It is shaped by memory, relationships, achievements, failures, culture, occupation, beliefs, and personal reflection.

Identity influences future behavior while remaining capable of change.

---

# Scientific Basis

Identity is a dynamic psychological construct formed through continuous interaction between the individual and society.

People define themselves through roles, values, beliefs, group membership, personal history, and aspirations.

Identity develops throughout life rather than appearing suddenly.

The Garden models identity as an emergent, continuously evolving cognitive system.
