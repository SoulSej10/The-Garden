# Purpose

Foundations of Intelligence establishes the conceptual architecture for every intelligent agent within The Garden.

An intelligent agent is an autonomous entity capable of perceiving its environment, maintaining internal state, making decisions, learning from experience, forming relationships, and pursuing goals over time.

This document defines what intelligence means within the simulation and provides the foundation for all future cognitive systems.

---

# Scientific Basis

Cognition emerges from the continuous interaction between an organism and its environment.

Intelligence is not a single ability.

It is the coordinated operation of multiple cognitive processes including perception, memory, emotion, learning, motivation, reasoning, and communication.

The Garden models intelligence as an emergent system composed of interacting cognitive modules rather than a centralized controller.

---

# Design Philosophy

Intelligence is not omniscience.

Agents never possess complete knowledge.

Every decision is made using incomplete information, imperfect memory, personal experience, and individual perception.

Mistakes are not failures.

They are an essential characteristic of intelligent behavior.

---

# Definition of an Intelligent Agent

An intelligent agent possesses the ability to:

Perceive.

Interpret.

Remember.

Decide.

Act.

Learn.

Adapt.

Communicate.

Maintain relationships.

Influence the world.

Be influenced by the world.

Without these abilities, an entity is reactive rather than cognitive.

# The Cognitive Loop

Every intelligent agent continuously operates through a repeating cognitive cycle.

```text
World Changes
      ↓
Perception
      ↓
Interpretation
      ↓
Memory
      ↓
Emotion
      ↓
Motivation
      ↓
Decision
      ↓
Action
      ↓
World Changes
```

The loop never truly stops while the agent is alive.

# Agency

Agency represents the capacity to choose between multiple possible actions.

Choice requires:

Alternatives.

Evaluation.

Preference.

Commitment.

Every action taken by an intelligent agent should originate from agency.

---

# Rationality

Agents are boundedly rational.

They do not calculate perfect solutions.

Instead they make sufficiently good decisions using:

Limited information.

Limited time.

Limited experience.

Limited computational resources.

This mirrors both biological intelligence and practical simulation requirements.

# Relationship to Civilization

Civilizations are not intelligent.

People are.

Kingdoms possess no mind of their own.

Every law, invention, war, friendship, migration, revolution, and discovery begins with the thoughts of individuals.

History emerges from minds interacting across generations.

Civilization is therefore an emergent property of collective intelligence rather than an independent system.

# Closing Statement

A mountain does not wonder.

A river does not hesitate.

A tree does not question tomorrow.

But one day, within the living world, a being looked beyond the present moment.

It remembered yesterday.

It imagined tomorrow.

It chose between possibilities.

That single act transformed the world forever.

The first intelligent thought did not merely change the individual who had it.

It created history.

In The Garden, intelligence is not defined by knowledge.

It is defined by the ability to perceive a world that is uncertain, imagine a future that does not yet exist, and choose a path through both.
