# Purpose

Personality defines the enduring behavioral tendencies that distinguish one intelligent individual from another.

Personality does not determine actions directly.

Instead, it continuously influences perception, emotional responses, motivation, learning, relationships, communication, and decision-making.

Every intelligent agent possesses a unique personality profile that evolves gradually throughout life.

---

# Scientific Basis

Modern personality psychology suggests that stable behavioral tendencies emerge from the interaction of biology, development, learning, environment, and lived experience.

While personality remains relatively stable, significant life events can gradually reshape aspects of an individual's character.

The Garden models personality as a dynamic but persistent cognitive system.

---

# Design Philosophy

No two minds experience the world in exactly the same way.

Personality is not a list of labels.

It is the lens through which reality is interpreted.

Identical circumstances do not create identical individuals.

# Behavioral Bias

Personality biases decision-making.

Examples include:

Curious individuals explore more.

Cautious individuals avoid unnecessary risk.

Empathetic individuals assist others more frequently.

Ambitious individuals pursue leadership opportunities.

Competitive individuals value achievement.

Personality influences probabilities rather than enforcing outcomes.

---

# Personality Stability

Most personality dimensions remain relatively stable over long periods.

However:

Major trauma.

Life-changing success.

Profound relationships.

Aging.

Long-term learning.

may gradually reshape behavioral tendencies.

Personality is persistent but not immutable.

---

# Individual Variation

Even siblings raised in the same household may develop different personalities.

Variation emerges from:

Unique experiences.

Different memories.

Distinct relationships.

Chance encounters.

Personal interpretation of events.

No two life histories are identical.

---

# Personality Expression

Internal personality may not always match outward behavior.

Examples include:

A fearful leader acting bravely.

A grieving parent remaining calm.

A shy scholar becoming confident while teaching.

Context influences expression.

# Simulation Rules

Personality evolves gradually.

Major life events may reshape personality over long periods.

Every decision is influenced—but never completely determined—by personality.

# Performance Considerations

Behavioral modifiers should influence existing cognitive systems rather than introducing independent decision logic.

Long-term personality evolution should occur gradually through significant experiences rather than routine daily events.

Two children stood before the same river.

One stepped into the current without hesitation.

One carefully searched for the safest crossing.

Neither choice was right.
