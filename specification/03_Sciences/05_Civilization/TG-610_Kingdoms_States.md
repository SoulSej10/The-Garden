# Purpose

Kingdoms and States define how civilizations organize territory, authority, institutions, and populations into persistent political entities.

A state is an emergent institution that coordinates large populations across space and time.

States are not created by the simulation.

They arise because growing civilizations require increasingly sophisticated coordination.

---

# Scientific Basis

Throughout history, states emerged when communities became too numerous and geographically dispersed to rely solely on informal leadership.

Agricultural surplus, permanent settlements, trade, defense, administration, taxation, law, and shared identity all contribute to state formation.

Different environments naturally produce different forms of governance.

There is no universal path toward statehood.

---

# Design Philosophy

Borders do not create civilizations.

Civilizations create borders.

A kingdom is simply a society that has learned to organize itself across great distances.

# Relationship to Civilization

Civilizations create states because coordination eventually exceeds the limits of personal relationships.

A farmer grows food hundreds of kilometers from the capital.

A judge settles disputes in a distant province.

An engineer builds a bridge for people they will never meet.

The state connects these strangers into a functioning whole.

Its true purpose is not power.

It is coordination.

---

# Closing Statement

A river separated two villages.

For generations they traded as neighbors.

One year they built a bridge.

The bridge became a road.

The road became a market.

The market attracted settlers.

Soon both villages elected the same council.

Years later they shared the same laws.

Centuries passed.

People no longer remembered when they had been separate.

In The Garden, kingdoms are not born from conquest alone.

They are born whenever enough people choose to build a future together.
