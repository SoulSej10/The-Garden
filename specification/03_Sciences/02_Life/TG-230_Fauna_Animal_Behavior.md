# Purpose

Fauna & Animal Behavior defines all non-sapient animals within The Garden.

Animals consume energy, move through the environment, reproduce, compete, cooperate, migrate, and continuously reshape ecosystems through their behavior.

The purpose of this system is not merely to simulate animals, but to establish the world's first autonomous decision-makers.

Every future intelligent being builds upon the behavioral principles introduced here.

---

# Scientific Basis

Animals survive by continuously making decisions.

These decisions are influenced by:

Internal biological needs.

Environmental conditions.

Social interactions.

Available resources.

Risk.

Opportunity.

The Garden models animal intelligence through adaptive behavioral systems rather than scripted actions.

---

# Design Philosophy

Animals do not follow scripts.

They pursue survival.

Every decision emerges from competing needs.

A hungry wolf hunts.

A frightened deer flees.

A nesting bird protects its young.

No action exists without motivation.

Behavior should emerge naturally from biological priorities.

```text id="0ql0gz"

        ↓

        ↓
Offspring Protection
        ↓

        ↓
Shelter
        ↓
Territory
        ↓
Social Behavior
        ↓

        ↓
```

# Relationship to Civilization

Long before people built kingdoms, animals already possessed societies.

Packs defended territory.

Birds migrated across continents.

Beavers reshaped rivers.

Elephants remembered ancient paths.

Civilization did not invent intelligence.

It inherited it.

The behaviors of future citizens are rooted in the survival strategies first expressed by the animals that came before them.

# Closing Statement

The wolf chooses to hunt.

The deer chooses to flee.

The bird chooses where to build its nest.

The whale chooses when to begin its migration.

None of these choices are random.

Each is a quiet answer to the oldest question in nature:

How do I survive one more day?

From those countless decisions, ecosystems emerge.

And from those ecosystems, the foundations of intelligence are born.

Animals are not moving decorations within The Garden.

They are the world's first thinkers.
