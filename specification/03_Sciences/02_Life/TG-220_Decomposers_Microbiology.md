# Purpose

Decomposers & Microbiology define the invisible biological systems responsible for recycling matter throughout The Garden.

Every fallen tree, dead animal, discarded crop, and decaying organism eventually returns to the ecosystem through decomposition.

Without decomposers, nutrients become trapped, ecosystems stagnate, and life gradually collapses.

This system closes the biological cycle.

---

# Scientific Basis

Decomposition is driven by fungi, bacteria, microorganisms, and detritivores.

These organisms break down organic material into nutrients that become available to plants and the wider biosphere.

The Garden models decomposition as a continuous ecological process rather than individual microscopic interactions.
