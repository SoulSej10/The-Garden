# Purpose

Language defines the shared symbolic system through which a society represents, stores, and transmits meaning.

While communication enables information exchange, language provides the structured vocabulary, grammar, and symbols that make complex thought possible.

Language allows knowledge to survive beyond the lifetime of any individual.

---

# Scientific Basis

Language is an evolving system of symbols used to encode meaning.

Languages naturally change over time through usage, innovation, migration, isolation, conquest, trade, and cultural exchange.

No language remains completely static.

The Garden models language as a living social system rather than a fixed dictionary.

---

# Design Philosophy

Words are tools.

Language is not merely a collection of words.

It is the shared framework that allows a civilization to think together.

As societies change, so too does their language.

# Performance Considerations

The simulation should model languages at the societal level rather than generating complete grammars or dictionaries.

Vocabulary growth, divergence, and mutual intelligibility should be represented through abstract metrics and historical events.

Language changes should occur gradually over generations rather than daily.

# Relationship to Civilization

Civilizations remember through language.

A law survives because it is written.

A discovery survives because it is described.

A religion survives because its stories are retold.

A kingdom survives because its history is recorded.

Language is the bridge between generations.

Without it, every generation begins again.

# Closing Statement

A child learned a word from their grandmother.

Years later, they taught it to their own grandchildren.

The pronunciation changed.

The meaning grew.

The story behind it became legend.

The grandmother was forgotten.

The word remained.

In The Garden, languages are living organisms.

They are born, they evolve, they divide, they influence one another, and sometimes they disappear.

But while they live, they carry the memory of entire civilizations.
