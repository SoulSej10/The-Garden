# Purpose

Education defines how societies intentionally preserve, organize, improve, and transmit knowledge across generations.

Education transforms individual learning into collective learning.

It allows civilizations to accumulate knowledge instead of repeatedly rediscovering it.

As educational systems mature, societies become increasingly capable of scientific, technological, cultural, and institutional advancement.

---

# Scientific Basis

Education is the structured transmission of knowledge, skills, values, and practices between individuals and generations.

It may occur formally or informally.

Learning within society progresses through observation, mentorship, apprenticeship, instruction, experimentation, and institutional education.

The Garden models education as a social infrastructure that increases the efficiency and reliability of knowledge transfer.

---

# Design Philosophy

Every generation stands on the shoulders of the previous one.

Education is the ladder that allows civilization to climb.

# Closing Statement

A master craftsperson spent forty years perfecting their work.

Before they died, they taught one apprentice.

That apprentice taught three more.

Generations passed.

The master's name disappeared.

Their techniques remained.

Cities rose from knowledge carried by hands that had never met the one who first discovered it.

In The Garden, civilizations become immortal not because people live forever, but because knowledge does.
