# Purpose

Economics defines how societies produce, distribute, exchange, consume, and preserve scarce resources.

It governs the movement of food, materials, labor, services, and wealth throughout civilization.

Economic systems emerge from countless local decisions rather than centralized design.

The Garden models economies as adaptive, decentralized networks driven by individual and collective behavior.

---

# Scientific Basis

Economics studies how individuals and groups make decisions under scarcity.

Every citizen possesses limited:

Time.

Energy.

Knowledge.

Resources.

Opportunities.

Individuals continuously choose how to allocate these scarce assets.

The accumulation of these decisions creates local and global economies.

---

# Design Philosophy

No civilization becomes wealthy because someone commands prosperity.

Prosperity emerges when millions of ordinary decisions reinforce one another.

Economies are living systems.

# Performance Considerations

Economic simulations should aggregate routine transactions while preserving historically significant economic events.

Local markets should summarize thousands of exchanges into regional economic indicators.

Only major shifts in production, trade, investment, and prosperity require long-term persistence.

# Relationship to Civilization

Civilizations are sustained by the invisible movement of resources.

A farmer harvests grain.

A miller grinds flour.

A baker bakes bread.

A merchant transports it.

A family eats.

None of them planned the entire chain.

Yet together they created prosperity.

Economics is civilization's circulatory system.

---

# Closing Statement

A farmer grew more grain than their family needed.

They traded the surplus for tools.

The blacksmith used those tools to forge better plows.

The improved plows increased the next harvest.

Neither person intended to transform their village.

Together, they did.

In The Garden, economies are not commanded.

They emerge from countless ordinary choices that, over time, become extraordinary civilizations.
