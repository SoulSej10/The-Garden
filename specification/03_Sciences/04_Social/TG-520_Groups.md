# Purpose

Groups define how individuals organize into stable social units for cooperation, survival, identity, and shared purpose.

Groups are emergent.

They are not placed into the world by the simulation.

They arise because individuals repeatedly interact, develop trust, and recognize mutual benefit.

Every institution within civilization ultimately begins as a group.

---

# Scientific Basis

Humans naturally organize into groups because cooperation increases survival, efficiency, security, and social belonging.

Groups develop internal structures, shared expectations, roles, norms, and identities.

Over time, groups become increasingly organized and may evolve into institutions.

The Garden models groups as dynamic social systems that continuously adapt to internal and external pressures.

---

# Design Philosophy

No civilization begins with a kingdom.

It begins with a handful of people choosing to remain together.

Every empire once started as a small group.
