# Purpose

Politics defines how societies make collective decisions, distribute authority, resolve competing interests, and organize public action.

Governance is an emergent response to coordination problems.

No political system exists at the beginning of civilization.

Institutions arise only when societies become sufficiently large and complex that informal coordination is no longer enough.

The Garden models politics as an evolving adaptive system rather than predefined governments.

---

# Scientific Basis

Political systems emerge wherever groups require shared decision-making.

Population growth, specialization, economic complexity, conflict, and resource management increase the need for organized governance.

Political institutions continuously evolve in response to changing social conditions.

# Relationship to Civilization

Civilizations grow beyond the scale of personal relationships.

A village can gather beneath a tree to decide together.

A nation cannot.

Politics develops because cooperation must continue even when every citizen cannot speak at once.

Government is civilization's method of organizing collective action.

# Closing Statement

One village gathered beneath an ancient oak whenever decisions needed to be made.

Years later, the village became a city.

The tree remained.

The meetings moved into a council hall.

Centuries later, no one remembered why the council chamber had been built in that place.

But every leader still entered through the same eastern door, honoring a tradition that began beneath a tree where neighbors once spoke as equals.

In The Garden, governments are not invented overnight.

They grow from conversations that became customs, customs that became institutions, and institutions that became civilization.
