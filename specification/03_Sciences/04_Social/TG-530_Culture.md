# Purpose

Culture defines the shared knowledge, values, traditions, customs, beliefs, practices, symbols, and behaviors that emerge within a society over time.

Culture is not created.

It accumulates.

Every generation inherits part of the past while adding something new.

Culture becomes the collective memory of society.

---

# Scientific Basis

Culture emerges through repeated social interaction across generations.

It is transmitted through communication, imitation, education, ritual, and shared experience.

Environmental pressures, historical events, technological progress, and contact with other societies continuously reshape culture.

The Garden models culture as an evolving adaptive system rather than a predefined template.

# Performance Considerations

Culture should be represented through evolving societal attributes and historical records rather than tracking every individual cultural action.

Long-lived traditions should be stored as persistent cultural objects linked to their origin and evolution.

Large cultural changes should emerge from accumulated historical pressures instead of instantaneous events.
