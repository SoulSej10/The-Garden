# Purpose

Social Norms define the unwritten expectations that regulate everyday behavior within a society.

Norms guide individuals by establishing what behaviors are considered appropriate, admirable, acceptable, questionable, or unacceptable.

Unlike formal laws, norms are enforced primarily through social approval and disapproval.

They allow societies to coordinate behavior without requiring constant authority.

---

# Scientific Basis

Norms emerge through repeated social interaction.

Successful behaviors become expected.

Expected behaviors become traditions.

Repeated traditions become social norms.

Norms evolve continuously as societies respond to changing environments, technologies, and historical events.

The Garden models norms as adaptive rather than permanent.

# Closing Statement

A traveler arrived at sunset.

No law required anyone to offer shelter.

An old woman invited the traveler inside.

Years later, her grandchildren did the same.

Eventually, every household did.

No one remembered who began the custom.
