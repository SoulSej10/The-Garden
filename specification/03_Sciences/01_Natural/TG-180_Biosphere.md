# Purpose

The Biosphere defines the living network of the planet.

It connects every ecosystem into a self-sustaining system capable of growth, adaptation, recovery, and decline.

Its purpose is to ensure that life continues independently of civilization.

Nature is not background scenery.

Nature is the world's oldest civilization.

---

# Scientific Basis

The Garden models the biosphere using simplified ecological principles.

Energy enters primarily through sunlight.

Matter cycles continuously through living organisms, soil, water, and atmosphere.

Populations influence one another through cooperation, competition, predation, and environmental feedback.

The simulation values believable ecological relationships over biological precision.

---

# Design Philosophy

Life creates more life.

Death creates new life.

Nothing exists in isolation.

Every organism changes its environment.

Every environment changes the organisms living within it.

The biosphere is a continuous conversation between living systems.

---

# Core Concepts

The biosphere is self-organizing.

No ecosystem requires direct intervention to survive.

Environmental equilibrium emerges naturally through countless local interactions.

Perfect balance rarely exists.

Continuous adjustment is normal.

# Relationship to Civilization

Civilizations exist within the biosphere.

They do not replace it.

Every settlement extracts energy from surrounding ecosystems.

Healthy civilizations maintain ecological balance.

Unsustainable civilizations gradually weaken the natural systems upon which they depend.

Eventually, the biosphere responds.

Sometimes through scarcity.

Sometimes through collapse.

Sometimes through recovery after civilization itself disappears.

Nature is always older.

Nature is always patient.

# Closing Statement

Long before the first village was built...

The forests were already growing.

The rivers were already flowing.

The predators were already hunting.

The flowers were already blooming.

The world did not begin with civilization.

It welcomed civilization into a living system that had existed for ages.

And long after the last kingdom has fallen, the forests may return, rivers may carve new valleys, and life may once again flourish where history has faded.

The biosphere is the enduring heartbeat of The Garden.

It does not belong to humanity.

Humanity belongs within it.
