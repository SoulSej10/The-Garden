# Purpose

Geology defines the physical structure of the planet.

It governs the formation, composition, stability, and gradual evolution of the land itself.

While the Planetary Model establishes where land exists, Geology explains why that land possesses its current form.

It is the framework upon which ecosystems, resources, and civilizations are built.

---

# Scientific Basis

The Garden models geological processes using simplified long-term simulation.

The objective is not to recreate real-world geophysics in full detail, but to provide believable terrain evolution and meaningful environmental constraints.

Geological change occurs on timescales far longer than biological or political systems.

---

# Design Philosophy

The land remembers.

Mountains outlive kingdoms.

Valleys preserve ancient rivers.

Cliffs resist empires.

Stone records time more faithfully than history.

Geology should provide permanence within a world that is otherwise constantly changing.

---

# Core Concepts

Geology is slow.

The land changes gradually.

Most geological features remain recognizable for thousands of simulated years.

Only extraordinary events significantly reshape the landscape.

# Terrain Stability

Every geological feature possesses stability.

Highly stable formations remain largely unchanged.

Less stable formations may experience:

Erosion

Rockfalls

Landslides

Coastal retreat

River carving

# Erosion

Erosion slowly reshapes terrain.

Primary causes include:

Flowing water

Wind

Freeze-thaw cycles

Vegetation loss

Extreme weather

Erosion is cumulative.

Individual events are small.

Centuries reveal their effects.

# Simulation Rules

Geological systems evolve slowly.

Rules include:

Elevation changes gradually.

Erosion accumulates over time.

Water accelerates terrain change.

Vegetation reduces erosion.

Steep slopes increase landslide probability.

Resource deposits remain finite unless renewable by design.

The land should appear ancient rather than frequently changing.

Static geological data should be heavily cached.

Mountains protect kingdoms.

Valleys feed populations.

Stone enables architecture.

Minerals drive industry.

# Closing Statement

Stone does not hurry.

Mountains are patient.

Rivers carve valleys one season at a time.

Cliffs endure storms without remembering them.

Civilizations rise and disappear.

Forests grow and burn.

Generations are forgotten.

Yet the land remains, carrying the silent memory of everything that has ever lived upon it.

Every kingdom builds its future upon foundations laid long before history began.
