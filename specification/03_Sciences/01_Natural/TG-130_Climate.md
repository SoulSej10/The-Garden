# Purpose

Climate defines the long-term environmental identity of a region.

Unlike weather, which changes from day to day, climate changes slowly over decades, centuries, and millennia.

Its purpose is to create stable environmental patterns that influence ecosystems, resource distribution, agriculture, wildlife, and civilization.

Climate provides continuity across generations.

---

# Scientific Basis

Climate is the statistical behavior of weather over long periods.

The Garden models climate as an emergent property produced by geography, seasons, atmospheric behavior, water systems, and long-term environmental feedback.

The simulation emphasizes believable regional identities rather than exact Earth-like climate modeling.

---

# Design Philosophy

Weather creates moments.

Climate creates identity.

Players should remember places because of their climate.

Some valleys become fertile.

Some mountains remain frozen.

Some plains suffer recurring drought.

These characteristics should emerge naturally from long-term simulation instead of being manually assigned whenever possible.

---

# Core Concepts

Climate changes slowly.

Climate influences weather probabilities.

Weather gradually reinforces climate.

Climate is regional.

Neighboring regions may possess dramatically different climates because of geography, elevation, or nearby water.

Climate forms the environmental memory of the planet.

---

# Climate Stability

Climate remains relatively stable.

Small yearly fluctuations are expected.

Major climatic shifts require sustained environmental change.

Examples include:

Centuries of drought.

Volcanic activity.

Large-scale deforestation.

Future civilization-driven environmental impact.

Climate should resist sudden change.

---

# Climate Influence

Climate directly influences:

Biome formation.

River persistence.

Forest density.

Crop suitability.

Animal habitats.

Disease prevalence.

Population density.

Settlement expansion.

Trade routes.

Construction materials.

Civilizations adapt to climate rather than the reverse.

---

# Seasonal Expression

Each climate experiences the same seasons differently.

Example:

Winter in a rainforest differs greatly from winter in the mountains.

Summer in a desert differs greatly from summer in fertile plains.

The calendar remains universal.

The experience of each season varies by climate.

# Simulation Rules

Climate evolves through accumulated environmental conditions.

Rules include:

Weather influences climate over long periods.

Single weather events cannot redefine climate.

Vegetation gradually reinforces regional climate.

Large environmental changes require long-term causes.

Climate possesses inertia.

# Performance Considerations

Climate updates occur infrequently compared to weather.

Most climatic values should be derived from long-term statistical trends rather than constant recalculation.

Regional aggregation should minimize computational cost.

# Relationship to Civilization

Climate quietly determines the destiny of civilizations.

It influences where cities are founded.

Which crops succeed.

Which resources become valuable.

Which trade routes emerge.

Which cultures develop traditions around rain, snow, rivers, or drought.

Empires do not choose their climate.

They learn to survive within it.

# Closing Statement

Weather is fleeting.

Climate endures.

A storm passes.

A season ends.

A generation grows old.

Yet the mountains remain cold.

The plains remain fertile.

The forests remain lush.

Climate is the long memory of the planet, quietly shaping every ecosystem, every migration, every harvest, and every civilization long before history begins to record their names.
