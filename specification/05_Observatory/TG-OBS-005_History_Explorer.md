# Purpose

This document defines the History Explorer, the Observatory's primary system for investigating the past.

The History Explorer is not merely a timeline.

It is an investigative environment that allows players to navigate historical events, understand causality, compare different eras, and reconstruct how the world evolved over time.

History should become explorable rather than simply readable.

---

# Core Principle

**History is another dimension of exploration.**

Players move through time just as naturally as they move through space.

The past is not a collection of records.

It is a landscape waiting to be explored.

---

# Design Philosophy

Every present-day observation should have a discoverable past.

Every historical event should reveal future consequences.

The History Explorer encourages players to investigate history through curiosity rather than passive reading.

Understanding emerges through exploration.

---

# Historical Navigation

Players may travel through:

Years.

Generations.

Historical Ages.

Civilizational eras.

Major historical events.

Personal lifetimes.

The player should always remain aware of both temporal and geographical context.

---

# Temporal Exploration

Players may investigate:

What existed.

What changed.

What disappeared.

What emerged.

What persisted.

History is experienced as continuous transformation rather than isolated moments.

---

# Causal Investigation

The History Explorer should reveal:

Causes.

Consequences.

Dependencies.

Ripple effects.

Long-term outcomes.

Players should be able to follow chains of historical influence across centuries.

---

Population.

# Historical Integrity

The History Explorer never rewrites history.

It presents events exactly as recorded by the simulation.

Interpretation belongs to the player.

The Observatory provides evidence.

Not conclusions.

# Closing Statement

A player noticed that a prosperous city stood in the middle of an otherwise barren plain.

They entered the History Explorer.

Five hundred years earlier, there had been no city.

Only a river.

Centuries before that, a glacier had carved the valley.

Generations later, fertile soil accumulated.

Farmers settled the land.

Roads connected villages.

Merchants followed.

A kingdom emerged.

The player closed the History Explorer.

The city looked the same as before.

But now they understood why it existed.

In The Garden, history is not a record of the past.

It is the explanation of the present.
