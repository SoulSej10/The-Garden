# Purpose

This document defines how the lives of individual citizens become meaningful stories within The Garden.

Character stories are not written.

They emerge through the interaction of personality, relationships, choices, opportunities, failures, and historical circumstances.

Every citizen has the potential to become historically significant.

Most will simply live meaningful lives.

Both outcomes are equally valuable.

---

# Core Principle

**Every life is a story.**

Historical importance is not a prerequisite for narrative significance.

A parent raising a family.

A teacher inspiring generations.

A farmer feeding a village.

A sailor exploring distant shores.

Each life contributes to the larger history of the world.

---

# Design Philosophy

The simulation should never decide that someone is a hero.

Instead, it should faithfully record their life.

If future generations remember them, that remembrance becomes part of history.

Greatness is recognized.

It is never assigned.

# Closing Statement

A young carpenter spent decades repairing homes after storms.

They never governed a kingdom.

Never commanded an army.

Never discovered a new science.

When they died, hundreds attended the funeral.

Years later, their grandchildren became engineers inspired by the craftsmanship they had witnessed growing up.

Centuries later, no record preserved the carpenter's name.

Yet the bridges their descendants built connected an entire continent.

In The Garden, a life does not become meaningful because history remembers it.

History becomes meaningful because countless lives quietly shaped it.
