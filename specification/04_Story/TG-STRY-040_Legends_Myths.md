# Purpose

This document defines how historical events gradually transform into legends, myths, folklore, and cultural traditions.

The Garden distinguishes between **what actually happened** and **what people believe happened**.

Objective history belongs to the simulation.

Legends belong to civilization.

---

# Core Principle

**Memory changes faster than history.**

Facts remain constant.

Stories evolve.

Each generation remembers only part of the past.

The missing pieces are filled by imagination, interpretation, belief, and tradition.

---

# Design Philosophy

No civilization intentionally creates myths.

Myths emerge because memory is imperfect.

An ordinary hunter may become a legendary giant slayer.

A devastating flood may become a tale of divine judgment.

A brilliant engineer may later be remembered as a magician.

Legends are history viewed through generations of retelling.

---

# Myth Formation

Legends may emerge from:

Historical heroes.

Great disasters.

Wars.

Scientific discoveries.

Exploration.

Founding events.

Natural phenomena.

Remarkable animals.

Ancient ruins.

Unexplained mysteries.

Every myth should trace back to real simulation events whenever possible.

---

# Cultural Evolution

Stories evolve over time through:

Oral tradition.

Language change.

Artistic interpretation.

Religious influence.

Political propaganda.

Incomplete historical records.

Generational retelling.

Different civilizations may tell entirely different versions of the same event.

---

# Legendary Figures

Some historical individuals become larger than life.

Possible transformations include:

Explorer → World Founder

General → Invincible Warrior

Scholar → Great Sage

Physician → Miracle Healer

Inventor → Master Artisan

Leader → Chosen Monarch

The Story System records both the historical individual and the legendary interpretation.

---

# Origin Stories

Civilizations naturally develop explanations for:

Their founding.

Sacred places.

Important traditions.

Ancient monuments.

National symbols.

Unique customs.

These stories may contain varying degrees of historical accuracy.

---

# Forgotten Truths

Some historical facts disappear entirely.

Reasons include:

Destroyed archives.

Lost languages.

Collapsed civilizations.

Political censorship.

Natural disasters.

Time.

The absence of evidence becomes fertile ground for new myths.

---

# Living Folklore

Folklore evolves continuously.

New stories appear.

Old stories merge.

Legends fade.

Heroes become symbols.

Every civilization develops its own narrative traditions.

---

# Historical Truth

The simulation always preserves:

Actual events.

True chronology.

Verified participants.

Original causes.

Authentic consequences.

Legends never overwrite objective history.

They exist alongside it.

# Closing Statement

Long ago, a bridge collapsed during a violent storm.

A young engineer rebuilt it using an unfamiliar design that resisted the river's current.

The bridge endured for centuries.

The engineer's name slowly disappeared from written records.

Generations later, children were told that a river spirit had blessed the bridge with eternal strength.

Pilgrims left offerings before crossing.

Poets sang of miracles.

Historians eventually uncovered fragments describing an ordinary engineer with extraordinary skill.

Neither story was entirely wrong.

One preserved the truth.

The other preserved its meaning.

In The Garden, history records what happened.

Legends record what people chose never to forget.
