# Purpose

This document defines how memories persist within The Garden after the events themselves have passed.

The simulation stores every event.

The world remembers only a small fraction.

World Memory determines which parts of history continue influencing future generations through culture, institutions, landscapes, artifacts, and collective remembrance.

---

# Core Principle

**The past never truly disappears.**

Every generation inherits a world shaped by those before it.

Some inherit written records.

Others inherit traditions.

Others inherit ruins.

Even forgotten events leave traces.

---

# Design Philosophy

History is not only remembered by people.

The world itself remembers.

A road remembers the merchants who built it.

A forest remembers the fire that reshaped it.

A river remembers centuries of engineering.

A ruined city remembers an empire.

Memory exists in both people and places.

# Closing Statement

A kingdom disappeared after centuries of prosperity.

Its people scattered.

Its language slowly vanished.

Its laws were forgotten.

Its palaces crumbled into stone.

Hundreds of years later, travelers still followed the ancient roads because they remained the easiest path through the mountains.

Children played among ruins whose builders no one could name.

Scholars uncovered fragments of forgotten books beneath collapsed libraries.

The kingdom was gone.

Its memory was everywhere.

In The Garden, civilizations may disappear.

The worlds they shaped never truly do.
