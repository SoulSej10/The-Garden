# Purpose

This document establishes the narrative philosophy of The Garden.

Unlike traditional games, The Garden does not create stories through scripted quests or predetermined plots.

Instead, stories emerge naturally from the interaction of living systems, autonomous individuals, historical events, and player observation.

The role of the simulation is not to write stories.

It is to create a world in which stories become inevitable.

---

# Core Principle

**Stories are discovered, not authored.**

The simulation never decides:

> "A hero shall appear."

Instead it creates the conditions in which someone may become one.

Likewise, no kingdom is destined to fall.

No civilization is destined to prosper.

History is allowed to unfold according to the same causal systems that govern the rest of the world.

---

# Design Philosophy

Every citizen believes they are living an ordinary life.

History remembers only a few of them.

A farmer does not know they are feeding the child who will unite kingdoms.

A teacher does not know the lesson they gave will inspire a scientific revolution.

A bridge builder does not know their work will become the center of an empire centuries later.

Narrative emerges because the future gives meaning to the past.

---

# What Constitutes a Story?

Within The Garden, a story is defined as:

> A sequence of causally connected events that acquires meaning through its consequences.

A story therefore requires:

* Participants.
* Decisions.
* Consequences.
* Context.
* Memory.

Without memory, events are merely occurrences.

Without consequences, events are merely moments.

---

# Narrative Emergence

Stories emerge from the interaction of:

Citizens.

Families.

Communities.

Civilizations.

Nature.

Conflict.

Cooperation.

Chance.

Player observation.

No single system owns the narrative.

Narrative is the observable pattern created by all systems together.

---

# Narrative Scales

Stories exist simultaneously at multiple scales.

Individual stories.

Family stories.

Community stories.

Regional stories.

Civilizational stories.

Planetary stories.

Every larger story contains countless smaller ones.

---

# The Role of the Player

The player is not the protagonist.

The player is the observer.

The player may influence the world.

They may intervene.

They may guide.

They may preserve.

But the world does not exist for the player's story.

The player's story exists because they witnessed the world's story.

---

# Historical Significance

Not every event deserves remembrance.

The simulation continuously evaluates which events meaningfully alter future history.

Only those events become part of the world's lasting narrative.

History is therefore curated by consequence rather than frequency.

---

# Emotional Attachment

Players become emotionally invested when they observe:

Growth.

Sacrifice.

Discovery.

Recovery.

Failure.

Hope.

Loss.

Legacy.

These emotions emerge naturally when individuals persist across time and influence future generations.

---

# Anti-Script Principle

The Garden shall never require:

Scripted heroes.

Mandatory villains.

Preset kingdoms.

Forced wars.

Artificial plot twists.

Guaranteed endings.

Every remarkable event should be explainable through the world's existing simulation.

---

# Narrative Persistence

Stories continue after individual participants disappear.

Ideas survive.

Buildings survive.

Laws survive.

Languages survive.

Cultures survive.

History survives.

Narrative therefore belongs to civilizations rather than individuals alone.

---

# Closing Statement

A child planted an acorn beside a stream.

Nothing remarkable happened.

Years passed.

The tree grew.

Travelers rested beneath its branches.

A village formed nearby.

Generations later, the village became the birthplace of a civilization.

Historians debated the founders.

Kings claimed the achievement.

Architects celebrated the city.

No record mentioned the child.

Yet without that tree, the village may never have existed.

In The Garden, stories are rarely about the moment something happened.

They are about the countless moments that became meaningful only because the future remembered them.
