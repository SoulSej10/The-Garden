# Purpose

This document defines how the player's actions become part of the world's ongoing history.

The player is not the protagonist.

They are another participant within a living world.

Their influence should be remembered according to its consequences rather than their identity as the player.

---

# Core Principle

**The world remembers actions, not privilege.**

The simulation never records an event because it was caused by the player.

It records events because they changed history.

If the player changes nothing, history continues.

If the player changes everything, history remembers accordingly.

---

# Design Philosophy

The player is not above the simulation.

They exist within it.

Like every civilization, family, and citizen, the player contributes to an evolving historical record.

The Garden does not distinguish between "player history" and "world history."

There is only history.

---

# Forms of Legacy

Players may leave legacies through:

Environmental restoration.

Scientific discovery.

Architectural achievement.

Historical preservation.

Diplomatic intervention.

Cultural influence.

Conservation.

Exploration.

Education.

Observation.

Legacy is defined by lasting consequence rather than immediate impact.

---

# Invisible Contributions

Some of the player's greatest contributions may never receive direct recognition.

Examples include:

Protecting an endangered species.

Preventing a famine.

Preserving an ancient ruin.

Saving historical records.

Guiding migration.

Restoring ecosystems.

Future generations simply inherit a better world.

---

# Shared History

Player actions become indistinguishable from the world's history.

Future historians may write:

"The Great Reforestation."

"The River Restoration."

"The Age of Exploration."

Without ever referring to an external player.

The world's history remains internally consistent.

---

# Multiple Players

Future versions of The Garden may support multiple observers.

Each participant contributes independently.

History records outcomes rather than ownership.

Legacy naturally becomes collaborative.

---

# Leaving the World

When the player exits:

The simulation continues.

Civilizations continue.

Nature continues.

History continues.

The player's influence remains only where consequences persist.

---

# Returning

Upon returning, the player may discover:

Cities transformed.

Children grown into elders.

Forests matured.

Empires collapsed.

Cultures evolved.

The world remembers through change rather than saved checkpoints.

---

# Ethical Legacy

The Story System never labels actions as inherently good or evil.

Instead, it records:

Consequences.

Influence.

Persistence.

Historical significance.

Different civilizations may interpret identical actions differently.

---

# Closing Statement

A traveler arrived in a quiet valley.

They planted forests where hills had become barren.

They repaired forgotten irrigation canals.

They protected a handful of endangered animals.

Then they departed.

Centuries passed.

Children played beneath towering trees.

Farmers harvested fertile fields.

Travelers crossed rivers that no longer flooded.

No monument bore the traveler's name.

No kingdom celebrated their arrival.

History simply described the valley as one of the most prosperous regions on the continent.

The traveler was forgotten.

Their work was not.

In The Garden, the greatest legacy is not being remembered.

It is creating a future where remembrance is no longer necessary.
